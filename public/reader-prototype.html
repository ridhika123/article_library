The AI That Rewrites
the Rules of Science

By Sarah Chen  ·  March 28, 2025  ·  8 min read

For the first time in decades, the scientific method itself is under review — not by philosophers, but by machines that have quietly begun forming hypotheses, designing experiments, and sometimes arriving at conclusions that surprise even their creators.

A New Kind of Collaborator

In a cluttered lab at MIT, a researcher named Daniel Park watches a screen as an AI system named Prometheus suggests a novel protein folding pathway. It's the third time this week the system has proposed something his team would never have considered.

"It doesn't think like us. That's exactly the point. We've been constrained by the limits of human intuition for centuries."

Prometheus is one of a new class of AI systems specifically designed not just to assist scientists, but to act as a scientific peer — one with access to the entire literature, perfect recall, and no attachment to prior beliefs.

Whether that makes it a better scientist than us, or just a very different one, is a question that may define the coming decade of research.
